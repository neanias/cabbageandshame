William Mathewson

Fri 09 January 2026 under Baker's Dozen

Squid - Houseplants

I appreciate that this is a single and an unusual entry in this list. Houseplants by Squid is the most important single in my personal musical journey. Nathan, Jon, Jake Hoyer-Miller and I all bought tickets to go to a day of All Points East in 2019, mainly because IDLES & Run the Jewels were playing that day. Nathan and I got to my family home in Suffolk before the others by some hours for train ticket price reasons and started looking through the line-up to work out who else we wanted to see besides IDLES & RTJ. We listened to a few singles for each band and rated them out of 10. As soon as we heard Houseplants, we were in love. We were obsessed. I’ve found the bullet point in my notes app from 30th May 2019: “Squid - totally weird, totally great; houseplants/10.”

The reason why this is the most important single in my personal musical journey is because this specific single is what got me deep into the then-nascent post-punk scene in the UK. The number of small gigs Nathan & I went to after that shot up, the number of monthly listeners of our main bands collapsed (rocking the <1k bands) and the active trajectory of what I was listening to changed, totally. From Squid, I got into Dry Cleaning, Sorry, Shame, Fontaines D.C. and Lord only knows how many others. Houseplants was my gateway drug and I will never kick the habit.
