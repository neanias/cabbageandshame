The Cool Greenhouse - The Cool Greenhouse

My favourite band. There’s just something about the wry lyricism and janky, simplistic guitars that really scratches an itch for me. I can’t describe it in any other way. At some point under lockdown (I think I was still living alone?) I spent the entire evening sitting on my massive bed reading articles about The Cool Greenhouse, mainly by the Quietus, and blasting their EPs. As you may remember, a lot of the Cababge & Shame episode devoted to The Cool Greenhouse was Nathan and I just reading out the lyrics and laughing. It was a particularly bright memory in an otherwise dull time of my life.

Once the album finally came out, I listened to it over and over through the summer, particularly in August. Dirty Glasses is probably my favourite track off this LP. I know the “dirty glasses” refer to the morals of the noted politicos in the song, but I prefer the idea of them actually being actual dirty glasses as it makes the imagery a lot funnier.

After we recorded the episode and tagged the band on Instagram, Tom Greenhouse replied to us about it and said they were also big fans of Yard Act and hoped to do something with them. Alas, I think Yard Act have rather outgrown such possibilities.
