William Mathewson

Fri 09 January 2026 under Baker's Dozen

Sorry - 925

This is the album that dislodged IDLES from my top spot in 2020. In fact, my year end top 10 albums didn’t have any IDLES albums. The change in my top albums from 2019 to 2020 is quite pronounced, with 925, Every Bad and West of Eden by HMLTD taking top billing. Wide Awake! was still in there at no.5, naturally. Of the big 2019-2020 music shake up, Sorry embedded deepest into my psyche as evidenced by the ~20 times I listened to it (digitally) that year. Seeing Sorry play Sneaky Pete’s about a month before they released their debut album definitely helped cement that. (That show later received a 3/5 rating in The Skinny saying the crowd were too sedate.)

As lockdown bit harder and harder, I was most drawn to gloomy, guitar-driven stuff, like 925 and Every Bad. What a surprise. Only after I read the book did I start to really break loose and add a lot more biting late ’70s and early ’80s post-punk. Similarly, the good weather cheered me up into albums like Twin Heavy by Willie J Healey and Making a New World by Field Music. Both 2 albums I really enjoy but don’t make the Baker’s Dozen cut.
